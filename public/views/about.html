Mini Autobiography

Ever since an early age, I have been heavily invested into computers and electronics. I played a TON of video games as a kid and I still do to this day. Sometime around grade eight I thought it would be cool to make a video game of my own, so I did! It was awful but I really enjoyed making it and I discovered my love for development and programming. I knew early on in high school that I wanted to pursue a career in software engineering so that's what I did. The University of Waterloo looked like it was full of tremendous opportunities. I pretty much knew I had to go there. Waterloo is very close to my home town too so that's where I set my sights. I have always excelled in school so I was accepted into the Software Engineering program without too much trouble. Since then, I have continued to remain successful in my studies. I have had three internships to date, all of which have been terrific. Two of them were at Sony based in Waterloo where I worked on both Media Go and the PlayStation Web Store. The other one was at Yelp in San Francisco working with the Internationalization team on cool experiment stuff related to their sign up page. Overall, I'm killing it and I'm very happy to be where I am (end humble brag).

Hobbies and Interests

Previously I stated that I play an exceptional amount of video games, but I also have other interests too. Somewhat recently, I have fell in love with juggling. At my first internship with Sony, compile times were kind of long so I found myself wanting to do something during those times. Some of my co-workers were juggling veterans so I picked it up myself. I'm still quite the amateur but I know a few 3-ball patterns and two 4-ball patterns. Another one of my hobbies is volleyball. I'm about 6'4" so I'm well suited for the sport and my favourite position is middle. I was captain of my school's volleyball team in grade twelve and I played on a rep volleyball team during my last three years of high school. My team travelled all around Ontario to compete. Nowadays I just play the sport casually when I get the chance. In high school, my classes weren't very compelling, so I became an origami connoisseur to help fill my time. I focused a lot on modular origami (origami where models are built out of several of the exact same piece) due to its heavy connections with geometry. Board games and card games are also things I have a passion for. I played tons of Magic: The Gathering during the Return to Ravnica block and won a few FNMs at my local game store but me (and my wallet) have taken a break from the activity, for now. I also like the board games Dominion and Settlers of Catan. Lastly, I play a little bit of the guitar, and although I enjoy it, I'm pretty bad.
